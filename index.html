The Opportunity
Three Forces Shaping IQVIA's Next Chapter

Based on your public filings, commercial product roadmap, and stated priorities, we see three interconnected strategic imperatives where enterprise AI infrastructure creates outsized leverage.

Force 1

The Commercial Solutions Replatform

Effective January 2026, IQVIA merged CSMS into TAS to form Commercial Solutions ($7.2B-$7.3B guided revenue). This is more than a reporting change. It signals an operating model shift toward integrated commercial engagement: data management, launch strategy, brand analytics, promotional engagement, and contract sales unified under one umbrella. Your commercial teams now need a single, permission-aware way to access knowledge across what were previously siloed business lines. That is a knowledge unification challenge at scale.

Force 2

The Agentic AI Imperative

IQVIA has deployed 150+ AI agents across 30+ use cases and named AWS its preferred agentic cloud provider. You have invested in the IQVIA.ai Platform, launched the Medical Reasoning LLM (Med-R1 8B), and partnered with NVIDIA to build custom healthcare-grade AI agents. The ambition is right. The next question is: how do you scale agent deployment across 93,000 employees without requiring each team to build from scratch? The bottleneck is rarely the model. It is getting the right enterprise context into the agent at the right time.

Force 3

The Knowledge Fragmentation Tax

IQVIA operates across clinical, commercial, and real-world evidence domains. Your teams navigate hundreds of internal systems, from Veeva Vault to SharePoint to proprietary analytics platforms. Every hour a field medical liaison spends searching for the latest brand strategy document, or a clinical operations lead spends tracking down a site-specific protocol deviation history, is an hour not spent on the work that drives your $32.7 billion backlog forward. Independent research shows knowledge workers lose 20-30% of their productive time to information retrieval. At IQVIA's scale, that represents thousands of FTEs worth of capacity.
